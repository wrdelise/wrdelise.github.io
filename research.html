The foci of my research are in analytic philosophy. If broadly, philosophy asks the question how things in the broadest possible sense of the term hang together in the broadest possible sense of the term, then at first blush, I am interested in reasons and their communicability between autonomous agents, and the structure of autonomous or heteronomous conceptual revision. I study the following disciplines in support of these questions.

  • Metaphysics
  • Philosophy of Language
  • Decision Theory
  • Meta Ethics

Some particular questions I have tried to answer include:

  • Are theories of personal identity communicable between theorists with differing ontologies, and if they are not, how ought they become communicable?
  • What concept of the analytic-synthetic distinction survives Grice and Strawson's reply to Quine's infamous Two Dogmas of Empiricism?
  • If philosophers of privacy are right to give individuals rights because they are part of a group, does the individual obtain any duties toward those groups or their members?
  • Are we sometimes wrong to dispense moral or rational prescriptions because we have good reasons to think them impossible?

Some questions that are on my mind include:

  • Can we change our concepts? If we can, should we? And when we do, is the change we are tracking a function of our meanings, or a function of our behaviors?
  • Is a desires as propositions theory of preferences coherent?
  • What notions is the Moral Nihilist tracking when using thick concepts?

Much of my background is in Classics. My distinct interests are in the ethnographical use of Fortuna-Tyche in Ammianus Marcellinus' Res Gestae and the implications of Greek and Latin semantics on the development of philosophical thought.
