I am a candidate for the Master's in philosophy at Columbia University. My current research interests are in metaphysics, (meta) ethics, reasons, action, and the philosophy of privacy broadly construed.

En route, I care a lot about the structure and communicability of reasons, the possibility and value of conceptual engineering projects, and how decision theory and philosophical logic constrain methods for good argumentation. My other substantial, but more personal, interests are in ancient philosophy, latin literature, translation theory, and language pedagogy.

I hail from Concord, Massachusetts and did my undergraduate work at Dalhousie University and the University of King's College, in Halifax Nova Scotia, where I received a B.A. with First Class Honors in both Philosophy and Classics.

You can find my C.V. and contact information at the top right; I am always open for discussion, whether on these topics, or on most anything else.
