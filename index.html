I'm studying for the philosophy MA at the CUNY Graduate Center. My research interests are in ethics, meta ethics (especially the theory of reasons), the philosophy of language (especially speech acts), decision theory, and the philosophy of privacy (especially the experience and value of privacy).

I hail from Concord, Massachusetts and did my undergraduate work at Dalhousie University and the University of King's College, in Halifax Nova Scotia. I graduated with honors in Philosophy and Classics.

Some questions I am thinking about currently include:

  • How should we understand contemporary attempts to explain the value of privacy in terms of autonomy (an experience) given that the extensions of feeling private and being private are not identical?
  • Are there some wrongheaded applications of the ought-implies-can objection?
  • How should we understand epistemic violence in the context of radical translation or ontological difference?
